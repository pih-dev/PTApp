Q4 — Leaf structure

A level alone, or a level × age band × gender cell? Determines how many classifications exist and how many programs the generator will need to produce later.

Option A

Level IS the classification

Age and gender are inputs that interpret the grades, but the output is just the level.

Evaluation ├── Beginner A (one leaf, one program) ├── Beginner B ├── Intermediate A ├── Intermediate B ├── Pro └── Elite
Example:
25-yr-old man with 15 pushups → "Average" → Beginner A
45-yr-old woman with 15 pushups → "Good" → Beginner A
→ Both follow the same "Beginner A" program.
Classifications: 6
Programs to build later: 6
pro Simpler data model, easier to maintain.
pro Only 6 program templates to write.
con 25-yr-old man and 55-yr-old woman do the identical daily plan.
con Recovery, volume, intensity can't adapt by age.
Option B

Level × Age × Gender = cell

Each leaf splits into a matrix. A classification is a tuple, not a single level.

Evaluation ├── Beginner A │ ├── M 20-29, F 20-29 │ ├── M 30-39, F 30-39 │ ├── M 40-49, F 40-49 │ └── ... (8 cells per leaf, TBD) ├── Beginner B (same split) ├── Intermediate A (same split) ├── Intermediate B (same split) ├── Pro (same split) └── Elite (same split)
Example:
25-yr-old man with 15 pushups → "Average" → Beginner A, Male, 20-29
45-yr-old woman with 15 pushups → "Good" → Beginner A, Female, 40-49
→ Different programs tuned to age/gender.
Classifications: 6 × N × 2 (e.g., 6 × 4 × 2 = 48 cells)
Programs to build later: many (deferred — but planner needs to know now)
pro Programs can adapt recovery/volume by age.
pro Matches Pierre's Input #1 wording ("classifications … based on 2 factors, age and gender").
con Many more program templates to write later.
con Age-band boundaries become a real decision (20-25? 20-29? 18-24?).
Claude's recommendation: B — Pierre's Input #1 phrased it as "classifications for all based on 2 factors, age and gender", plural. Program generator is deferred but this decision shapes the schema we build now.
